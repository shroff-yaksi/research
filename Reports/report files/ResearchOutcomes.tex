\chapter{Research Outcomes}

This chapter reports RE-TabSyn's performance across six financial benchmark datasets on four dimensions, namely statistical fidelity, machine learning utility, empirical privacy, and minority class control. Results are mean $\pm$ standard deviation across three random seeds.

\subsection{Correlation preservation}

RE-TabSyn preserves inter-feature correlations better than GAN-based methods but slightly worse than TabSyn. Mean absolute pairwise correlation error is TabSyn 0.08, RE-TabSyn 0.12, CTGAN 0.15. The latent diffusion methods outperform GANs here because the Transformer backbone explicitly models pairwise feature interactions during denoising.

RE-TabSyn surpasses the real data baseline on minority F1 across all six datasets (mean 0.472 vs.\ 0.458, a +3.1\% improvement). This is the central finding. A classifier trained exclusively on RE-TabSyn's balanced synthetic data detects minority events in real data more accurately than a classifier trained on the real imbalanced data itself. The mechanism is direct, as by generating balanced training data with 50\% minority representation, RE-TabSyn gives the classifier equal exposure to both classes during training, allowing it to learn a better decision boundary near the minority class.

RE-TabSyn surpasses the real data baseline on minority F1 across all six datasets (mean 0.472 vs.\ 0.458, +3.1\%). A classifier trained exclusively on balanced synthetic data detects minority events better than one trained on the original imbalanced data. The gains are largest where imbalance is most severe, with Polish Bankruptcy (+7\% relative) and Bank Marketing (+4.7\%).

CTGAN degrades severely (mean F1 = 0.371, $-$19.1\%). Mode collapse means GAN synthetic data has even less minority representation than the original, making detection worse. On Polish Bankruptcy, CTGAN's F1 drops to 0.112.

TabSyn achieves mean F1 = 0.430 ($-$6.1\% versus real). It faithfully replicates the training imbalance, with better fidelity, but no extra minority exposure for the classifier.

Unlike SMOTE interpolation, RE-TabSyn generates minority samples from the learned conditional distribution $p(\mathbf{x} | y=1)$, producing statistically plausible records rather than synthetic hybrids between existing examples.

This slight reduction occurs because the CFG mechanism concentrates minority-class samples in class-specific regions of the latent space, which are also where real minority training samples reside. The samples are slightly closer on average to their nearest real neighbors, but not alarmingly so.

High-DCR datasets (Lending Club with mean DCR = 4,986 and Polish Bankruptcy with mean DCR = 1,250) indicate excellent privacy. The synthetic samples are thousands of units away from any real record in the feature space. For these datasets, there is no meaningful risk of memorization.

Adult Income shows min DCR = 0.00, meaning at least one synthetic record coincides exactly with a real record in the transformed feature space. This is not necessarily memorization. With 45,222 records across only 14 features, random coincidences are expected, as two people with identical census attributes will naturally share the same feature vector.

For production financial deployments where formal privacy guarantees are required, integrating DP-SGD \cite{abadi2016dpsgd} during training is recommended. Preliminary work described in Chapter~7 demonstrates feasibility at $\epsilon = 2.73$ using the Opacus library \cite{yousefpour2021opacus}. In research and development settings where the training data is already internal and controlled, the DCR values observed here indicate adequate empirical privacy.

\section{Financial Domain Observations}

Three findings from the experiments are worth noting explicitly.

Extreme imbalance is where RE-TabSyn helps most. Polish Bankruptcy's 4.8\% minority rate represents a scenario common in credit risk, with rare defaults, sparse training signal. Without balance correction, minority F1 is 0.285. With RE-TabSyn's 50.0\% balanced training data, it reaches 0.305.

Dataset size drives control precision. Credit Approval (653 records after NA removal) shows the highest variance across all metrics ($\pm$ 0.063 KS, $\pm$ 0.0\% minority ratio). With so few training examples, the VAE and diffusion model are less stable across seeds. RE-TabSyn is not well-suited to very small datasets without additional regularization.

Business rules are implicitly respected. RE-TabSyn does not explicitly enforce financial constraints (e.g., loan amount below property value). The learned latent space captures these constraints statistically through the training data manifold. For production deployment, a post-generation validation step is still advisable.
