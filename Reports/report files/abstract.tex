Predictive modeling in the financial sector is severely hindered by class imbalance, where critical events such as fraud, loan defaults, and bankruptcies are exceedingly rare. Standard generative methods---CTGAN, TVAE, and TabSyn---faithfully replicate these skewed distributions, while direct diffusion approaches like TabDDPM fail catastrophically on mixed-type tabular data (KS $>$ 0.80).

We present \textbf{RE-TabSyn (Rare-Event Enhanced Tabular Synthesis)}, a novel framework that utilizes Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG) with latent diffusion models to enable explicit, inference-time control over synthetic class proportions. RE-TabSyn combines three components: (1) a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) encoding heterogeneous tabular features into a continuous latent space, (2) a Transformer-based Latent Diffusion Model for high-fidelity generation, and (3) a Classifier-Free Guidance mechanism allowing users to specify target minority ratios via a single guidance scale parameter $w$. This represents the \textbf{first application of CFG to tabular data synthesis}, confirmed through systematic review of 151 research papers.

Evaluated across six financial benchmark datasets with minority ratios ranging from 4.8\% to 44.5\%, RE-TabSyn demonstrates three key findings: (1) reliable generation of balanced datasets ($\sim$50\% minority, within 1.7\% of target on average), boosting minority representation by up to 10$\times$; (2) classifiers trained on RE-TabSyn's balanced synthetic data achieve an average minority F1-score of 0.472, \textbf{surpassing the real data baseline} of 0.458, despite a controlled fidelity trade-off (KS: 0.109 $\rightarrow$ 0.171); and (3) no systematic memorization (DCR $>$ 1.0 across all datasets). The core findings have been accepted at the \textbf{I2IT International Conference}.
